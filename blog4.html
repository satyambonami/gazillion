Reaching a Wider Audience with Your YouTube Video Through Click-Through Optimization

Alvin Colins.

Project Manager, Gazillion Expressions

According to several research and the numbers available, without a doubt, YouTube is currently the number one place to watch videos online. A glance at the numbers confirms this:

  • YouTube is the second biggest search engine, after Google

  • 74% of Americans tune in every month

  • YouTube has over 2 Billion users around the world

People all over the world are drawn to YouTube for different purposes, whether it’s to search for a solution to a problem, entertain themselves, or simply relax. Therefore, no matter what your business is about, you have an audience on YouTube.

In this YouTube SEO guide, we will be exploring one of the ways to rank your video on YouTube so your videos can enjoy a wider reach.

Google search’s algorithm is very similar to that of YouTube. Several factors contribute to a high-ranking video; one of such is Click-Through Optimization.

Click-Through Optimization

Every time someone watches a video on YouTube, the platform calculates what is called a click-through rate. In a layman’s language, click-through rate (CTR) is the percentage number of people that start watching a video after seeing its thumbnail on YouTube.
Below is a simple way to calculate the CTR of a video

CTR = (Clicks/Video Impressions) x 100

In the above example, you can see the CTR for one of our webinars, The PR Woman is reported as 5.8%.

However, this is simply an average CTR. Your CTR can vary widely based on certain factors, namely, your number of subscribers, your number of views, your niches, and how long the video has been uploaded to YouTube. The goal should be to get your CTR to double figures over time.
